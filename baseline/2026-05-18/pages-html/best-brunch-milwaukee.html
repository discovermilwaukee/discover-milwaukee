Best Brunch in Milwaukee

Brunch in Milwaukee isn't just a meal—it's a weekend ritual. From stacked pancakes to Bloody Mary bars, here's where to do it right.

IN THIS GUIDE

Top Brunch Spots in Milwaukee

These are the heavy hitters—the spots that have earned their reputation. Expect crowds on weekends, but the food justifies the hype.

Blue's Egg

$$West Side

Mon-Fri 7am-2pm, Sat-Sun 7am-3pm

Typical wait: 30-60 min weekends

The undisputed king of Milwaukee brunch. Blue's Egg has earned its legendary status with creative takes on breakfast classics and portions that mean business. The menu changes seasonally, but the quality never wavers. Yes, there's usually a wait on weekends—and yes, it's worth it.

MUST TRY

Seasonal Benedicts, Blue's Scramble, Any pancake special, Bloody Mary

THE VIBE

Buzzing, energetic, locals-know-it, family-friendly

Pro tip: Go on a weekday to skip the line. Or put your name in and grab coffee at Stone Creek next door.

317 N 76th St, Milwaukee, WI 53213

Story Hill BKC

$$$Story Hill

Sat-Sun 9am-2pm (brunch only)

Typical wait: 15-30 min weekends

Elevated brunch with high-quality ingredients and a refined menu. Story Hill BKC brings the same attention to detail from their dinner service to the weekend brunch table. Think locally-sourced eggs, house-made pastries, and cocktails that aren't an afterthought.

MUST TRY

Steak & Eggs, Brioche French Toast, Breakfast Burger, Mimosa Flight

THE VIBE

Upscale casual, date-worthy, refined but relaxed

Pro tip: Reservations accepted for brunch—use them.

5100 W Bluemound Rd, Milwaukee, WI 53208

Café Benelux

$$Third Ward

Sat-Sun 9am-3pm

Typical wait: 20-45 min weekends

A Third Ward staple offering a lively brunch scene and prime people-watching. The rooftop is the move in warmer months. Belgian-inspired menu with plenty of American brunch classics alongside European touches.

MUST TRY

Belgian Waffles, Croque Madame, Shakshuka, Rooftop Bloody Mary

THE VIBE

Trendy, social scene, great for groups, patio vibes

Pro tip: The rooftop opens early in spring—get there before the rush.

346 N Broadway, Milwaukee, WI 53202

Hidden Gem Brunch Spots

Skip the long lines and explore these under-the-radar favorites. Locals know, but the secret's getting out.

Engine Company No. 3

$$Walker's Point

Housed in a converted firehouse, this neighborhood gem serves consistently excellent brunch in a unique setting. Less crowded than the usual suspects but just as good.

MUST TRY

Fried Chicken & Waffles, Huevos Rancheros, Hash Browns

THE VIBE

Quirky, neighborhood feel, conversation-friendly

217 W National Ave, Milwaukee, WI 53204

Honeypie Café

$$Bay View

Bay View's comfort food destination. Honeypie delivers homestyle breakfast and brunch with serious pie credentials. Everything feels homemade because it is.

MUST TRY

Any pie slice, Biscuits & Gravy, Breakfast specials

THE VIBE

Cozy, homestyle, neighborhood hangout

2643 S Kinnickinnic Ave, Milwaukee, WI 53207

Palomino

$$Bay View

A bar that happens to serve excellent brunch. Palomino's weekend offerings are casual, affordable, and surprisingly good. Great bloody marys and a laid-back vibe.

MUST TRY

Build-Your-Own Bloody Mary, Breakfast Burrito, Eggs Benedict

THE VIBE

Casual, dive-y in the best way, no pretense

2491 S Superior St, Milwaukee, WI 53207

Glorioso's Italian Market

$East Side

Not a restaurant but a must-mention: Glorioso's deli counter serves incredible Italian breakfast sandwiches and espresso. Grab and go or eat at the small counter.

MUST TRY

Italian Breakfast Sandwich, Espresso, Fresh pastries

THE VIBE

Quick, authentic, no-frills delicious

1020 E Brady St, Milwaukee, WI 53202

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best brunch spot in Milwaukee?

Blue's Egg is consistently rated Milwaukee's best brunch spot, known for creative dishes and generous portions. Story Hill BKC offers a more upscale experience, while Café Benelux is perfect for those wanting a trendy Third Ward atmosphere with rooftop dining.

Where can I get brunch in Milwaukee without waiting?

To avoid brunch waits, try Story Hill BKC (accepts reservations), Engine Company No. 3 (less crowded), or go to popular spots on weekdays. Blue's Egg and Café Benelux typically have 30-60 minute waits on weekend mornings.

What time does brunch start in Milwaukee?

Most Milwaukee brunch spots open between 7am-9am on weekends. Blue's Egg opens at 7am, Story Hill BKC starts brunch at 9am. Many spots serve brunch until 2pm-3pm, with some offering all-day breakfast.

Where is the best Bloody Mary brunch in Milwaukee?

Milwaukee takes its Bloody Marys seriously. Top spots include Sobelmans (famous for over-the-top garnishes), Blue's Egg (quality classics), and Palomino (build-your-own bar). Café Benelux's rooftop bloodys are also popular in summer.

What Milwaukee neighborhoods are best for brunch?

Bay View offers multiple great options (Honeypie, Palomino, Odd Duck), Third Ward has the scene (Café Benelux, Café Hollander), and the East Side has quick options (Glorioso's, Beans & Barley). Blue's Egg on the West Side is worth the drive.
