Best Ice Cream & Frozen Custard in Milwaukee: The Frozen Custard Capital

Milwaukee is the unofficial frozen custard capital of the world, with more custard stands than any other city. The tradition runs deep—some shops have been serving the same creamy recipe for nearly 90 years.

Beyond custard, the city also offers artisanal ice cream shops pushing creative boundaries. This guide covers both traditions—from neon-lit custard stands to small-batch creameries. All locations verified open in 2026.

Last updated: April 2026 | All locations verified open

Classic Frozen Custard

Milwaukee's signature frozen treat

Kopp's Frozen Custard

Glendale / Greenfield / Brookfield$

Wisconsin Institution

The frozen custard institution of Milwaukee with a cult following. Kopp's features two flavors of the day alongside vanilla and chocolate, plus giant charbroiled burgers. The rotating flavors are legendary—fans track the calendar religiously.

Must Try: Flavor of the day, jumbo burger combo, sundaes
Best For: Wisconsin tradition, burger + custard combos, flavor variety
Insider Tip: Check the flavor calendar online and plan your visit around a special flavor. Some only appear a few times a year.

Multiple locations: 7631 W Layton Ave, 5373 N Port Washington Rd, 18880 W Bluemound Rd | All locations open 2026

Leon's Frozen Custard

South Side$

Neon-Lit Classic

The neon lights of Leon's have been beckoning Milwaukeeans since 1942. This drive-up institution serves creamy frozen custard in a setting that feels frozen in time—in the best way possible.

Must Try: Vanilla custard, chocolate custard, sundaes
Best For: Historic atmosphere, classic custard, photo ops
Insider Tip: The neon sign is iconic Milwaukee. Come at night for the full experience.

3131 S 27th St, Milwaukee, WI 53215 | Open 2026

Gilles Frozen Custard

Multiple Locations$

Milwaukee's Oldest

Milwaukee's oldest custard stand, operating since 1938. Gilles has been serving the same quality frozen custard for nearly 90 years, making it a living piece of Milwaukee history.

Must Try: Classic vanilla, chocolate, simple sundaes
Best For: History lovers, classic preparation, traditional experience
Insider Tip: The simplicity is the point. This is custard the way it's been done for generations.

7515 W Blue Mound Rd, Wauwatosa, WI 53213 | Open since 1938

Oscar's Frozen Custard

Waukesha / Franklin / Milwaukee$

Flavor of the Day Focus

With three locations, Oscar's has built a following for fun flavors of the day, charbroiled burgers, shakes, and the sundae of the month. The expanding footprint proves Milwaukee's custard appetite is endless.

Must Try: Flavor of the day, charbroiled burger, sundae of the month
Best For: Flavor variety, burger combos, suburban convenience
Insider Tip: The sundae of the month is worth following. Creative combinations change regularly.

Multiple locations throughout greater Milwaukee | All locations open 2026

Artisanal Ice Cream

Small-batch and craft options

Purple Door Ice Cream

Third Ward / Walker's Point$$

Small-Batch Artisanal

Milwaukee's premier small-batch ice cream maker with 34 rotating flavors. The Third Ward location is perfect for strolling, and the creative flavors go far beyond standard offerings.

Must Try: Seasonal specials, unique flavors, classic done right
Best For: Artisanal quality, unique flavors, Third Ward visits
Insider Tip: The rotating flavors mean there's always something new. Ask what's seasonal.

205 S 2nd St, Milwaukee, WI 53204 | Open 2026

Small-batch craft ice cream with creative flavors and quality ingredients. Scratch represents the modern ice cream movement with attention to sourcing and technique.

Must Try: Seasonal flavors, creative combinations
Best For: Craft ice cream, unique flavors, quality ingredients
Insider Tip: Follow for limited-time flavors. They experiment with interesting combinations.

6502 W North Ave, Wauwatosa, WI 53213 | Open 2026

Milwaukee Custard Facts

  • Milwaukee is home to the world's largest concentration of frozen custard shops, earning the unofficial title of 'frozen custard capital of the world.'
  • Frozen custard differs from ice cream by containing egg yolks and being served at a warmer temperature with less air whipped in.
  • Kopp's flavor calendar has inspired a devoted following—some flavors only appear a few times a year.
  • Leon's neon sign has been a Milwaukee landmark since 1942, appearing in films and representing the city's custard culture.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between frozen custard and ice cream?

Frozen custard contains egg yolks (at least 1.4% by weight), is served at a warmer temperature (around 18°F vs 10°F for ice cream), and has less air whipped in, making it denser and creamier.

What's the best frozen custard in Milwaukee?

Kopp's is the most popular for its flavor variety and burger combos. Leon's offers the most nostalgic atmosphere. Gilles is the oldest (since 1938). Each has devoted fans.

Where can I find artisanal ice cream in Milwaukee?

Purple Door Ice Cream in the Third Ward offers 34 rotating small-batch flavors. Scratch Ice Cream focuses on craft preparations with creative combinations.

Is frozen custard a Milwaukee thing?

While frozen custard originated elsewhere, Milwaukee has more custard stands than any other city, earning it the title of 'frozen custard capital of the world.'

What should I order at a Milwaukee custard stand?

The 'flavor of the day' is the classic order—a rotating specialty flavor alongside vanilla and chocolate. Sundaes and 'concrete mixers' (blended with toppings) are also popular.
