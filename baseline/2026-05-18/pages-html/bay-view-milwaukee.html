Welcome to Bay View

Bay View is Milwaukee's answer to Brooklyn—a neighborhood where artists, musicians, chefs, and young professionals have created something genuinely special. Located along the lakefront south of downtown, this former working-class neighborhood has transformed into the city's most creative enclave while maintaining its unpretentious, welcoming character.

The heart of Bay View is Kinnickinnic Avenue (locals call it "KK Ave"), a walkable stretch of independent restaurants, vintage shops, coffee roasters, and bars. Unlike Milwaukee's more polished Third Ward, Bay View has an edge—murals cover building sides, bands play in small venues, and conversation flows easily between strangers at neighborhood bars.

Add in South Shore Park with its lakefront beach, beer garden, and stunning views, and you've got a neighborhood that offers everything you need without ever getting in a car. It's no wonder this is where Milwaukee's tastemakers choose to live.

Best Bars in Bay View

South Shore Park & the Lakefront

Bay View's crown jewel is its lakefront access. South Shore Park offers beaches, trails, and one of Milwaukee's best beer gardens—all with stunning Lake Michigan views.

South Shore Park Highlights

SOUTH SHORE BEACH

One of Milwaukee's best urban beaches. Swim, sunbathe, or just watch the sailboats. Less crowded than Bradford Beach with more of a neighborhood feel.

BEER GARDEN

The South Shore Terrace beer garden offers cold beers and stunning lake views. Perfect for summer sunsets. Operated by the county parks system.

OAK LEAF TRAIL

The Oak Leaf Trail runs right through, connecting Bay View to downtown and points south. Perfect for running, biking, or a lakefront walk.

PAVILION & EVENTS

The historic pavilion hosts summer concerts and community events. Check the county parks calendar for what's happening during your visit.

Bay View Street Art & Murals

Bay View's creative spirit shows on its walls. Murals cover building sides throughout the neighborhood, turning a casual walk into an outdoor gallery experience.

Self-Guided Mural Walk

Start at Kinnickinnic & Lincoln and walk south on KK Ave. Keep your eyes on building sides, alleys, and parking lots—murals pop up everywhere. Key spots include:

  • The large-scale murals near Odd Duck
  • Colorful pieces in the alley behind Colectivo
  • Community murals on Potter Ave
  • The rotating walls near Sugar Maple

The neighborhood adds new murals regularly, so even repeat visitors will find something new.

Bay View also hosts Bay View Gallery Night several times a year, when neighborhood galleries, studios, and businesses open their doors for a community art walk. Check local listings for upcoming dates.

Frequently Asked Questions About Bay View

What is Bay View Milwaukee known for?

Bay View is Milwaukee's hip, artsy neighborhood known for its creative dining scene (Odd Duck, Honeypie, Birch), vintage shopping on Kinnickinnic Avenue, craft beer bars like Sugar Maple, vibrant street art, South Shore Park's lakefront access, and a thriving community of artists, musicians, and young professionals. It's often called Milwaukee's Brooklyn.

Is Bay View a good neighborhood to live in Milwaukee?

Bay View is one of Milwaukee's most desirable neighborhoods, especially for young professionals, artists, and families seeking walkability. It offers excellent restaurants and bars, Lake Michigan access, safe streets, strong community involvement, and a creative atmosphere. Housing ranges from historic bungalows to newer condos, with prices reflecting its popularity.

How do I get to Bay View Milwaukee?

Bay View is located south of downtown Milwaukee along the lakefront. From downtown, take I-794 East to the Lincoln Memorial Drive exit, or drive south on 1st Street. The neighborhood centers around Kinnickinnic Avenue (KK Ave) between Oklahoma Avenue and Lincoln Avenue. Street parking is generally available, and the neighborhood is very walkable once you arrive.

What is Kinnickinnic Avenue in Bay View?

Kinnickinnic Avenue (known locally as 'KK Ave') is Bay View's main commercial street and the heart of the neighborhood. This walkable stretch features the area's best restaurants (Odd Duck, Honeypie, Palomino), vintage shops, coffee roasters (Colectivo, Stone Creek), bars, and local boutiques. It's the best place to experience Bay View's creative, independent spirit.

What are the best restaurants in Bay View Milwaukee?

Bay View's top restaurants include Odd Duck (globally-inspired small plates), Honeypie Cafe (comfort food and legendary pies), Birch (upscale tasting menu), and Palomino Bar (elevated bar food). The neighborhood is known for chef-driven, creative cuisine that's less formal than downtown but equally delicious.

Is Bay View safe for visitors?

Bay View is considered one of Milwaukee's safest and most welcoming neighborhoods. The main commercial areas along Kinnickinnic Avenue are well-lit and busy with locals at all hours. Like any urban area, standard city awareness applies, but visitors consistently find Bay View friendly and comfortable to explore.
