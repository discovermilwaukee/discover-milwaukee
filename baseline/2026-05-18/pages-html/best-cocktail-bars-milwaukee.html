15 Best Cocktail Bars in Milwaukee: From Historic to Hidden

Milwaukee's cocktail scene goes far beyond brandy Old Fashioneds (though those are great too). From Bryant's—the city's oldest cocktail lounge where the Pink Squirrel was invented—to hidden speakeasies accessed through bookcases, the city offers sophisticated drinking for every taste.

This guide covers the best cocktail bars Milwaukee has to offer, from historic institutions to modern craft cocktail programs. All bars verified open in 2026.

Speakeasies & Hidden Bars

Secret entrances and intimate atmospheres

Bryant's Cocktail Lounge

Mitchell Street$$-$$$

Historic Cocktail Lounge | Intimate, moody, historic

Milwaukee's oldest cocktail lounge, operating since 1938. The exotic lighting, plush velvet walls, and no-menu tradition create an intimate atmosphere. Tell the bartender what you like, and they'll craft something perfect. Home of the Pink Squirrel.

Must Try: Pink Squirrel (invented here), Banshee, bartender's choice
Best For: Cocktail history, intimate dates, old-school Milwaukee
Insider Tip: There's no cocktail menu—describe your preferences and trust the bartender. It's how it's done here.

1579 S 9th St, Milwaukee, WI 53204

The Sofie

Downtown$$$

Modern Speakeasy | Sophisticated, energetic, glamorous

A chic, high-energy cocktail lounge with a speakeasy vibe in downtown Milwaukee. The Sofie combines craft cocktails with a sophisticated atmosphere and VIP seating options for those who want an elevated night out.

Must Try: Signature cocktails, champagne, seasonal creations
Best For: Special occasions, groups, upscale night out
Insider Tip: Reserve VIP seating for special occasions. The vibe gets lively later in the evening.

777 N Jefferson St, Milwaukee, WI 53202

Shanghai

East Side$$-$$$

Hidden Speakeasy | Mysterious, intimate, eclectic

A hidden speakeasy accessed through Black Cat Alley. Shanghai specializes in craft cocktails with a wide selection of absinthe—one of the best in the Midwest. The hidden entrance adds to the mystique.

Must Try: Absinthe service, classic cocktails, seasonal menu
Best For: Absinthe lovers, secret bar seekers, adventurous drinkers
Insider Tip: Finding the entrance is part of the experience. Look for the door in Black Cat Alley.

A haven for cocktail aficionados featuring exceptional drinks in a refined setting. Station No. 06 showcases quality ingredients and innovative techniques that put it among the best cocktail bars in the region. Winner of 'Best Cocktail Bar, Best of the Burbs' award.

Milwaukee Cocktail Culture

The Brandy Old Fashioned

Wisconsin's signature cocktail uses brandy (not bourbon), muddled fruit, and sweet or sour mix. Ask for it "sweet" or "sour"—or "press" for half 7-Up, half soda water.

The Pink Squirrel

Invented at Bryant's Cocktail Lounge in Milwaukee, this creamy pink drink combines crème de noyaux, crème de cacao, and cream. A must-try at its birthplace.

No-Menu Bars

Several Milwaukee bars, including Bryant's, operate without menus. Tell the bartender what flavors you like, and they'll craft something perfect. Trust the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best cocktail bar in Milwaukee?

Bryant's Cocktail Lounge is the historic choice—Milwaukee's oldest cocktail lounge. For modern craft cocktails, Station No. 06 and Edith are top picks. For speakeasy vibes, Shanghai and The Sofie deliver.

Where can I find speakeasy bars in Milwaukee?

Shanghai (hidden in Black Cat Alley), Urban Underground (through a bookcase), Mosler's Vault (in a bank vault), and The Sofie all offer speakeasy experiences with varying levels of 'hiddenness.'

What's Bryant's Cocktail Lounge known for?

Bryant's has operated since 1938 and invented the Pink Squirrel. There's no menu—you describe what you like and the bartender crafts something to your taste. It's Milwaukee cocktail history.

Best cocktail bar for a date in Milwaukee?

Bryant's Cocktail Lounge offers intimate, historic atmosphere. At Random provides unique retro charm. The Sofie works for a glamorous night out. Shanghai's hidden entrance adds adventure.

Where can I get good Japanese cocktails in Milwaukee?

Red Maple MKE specializes in Japanese-style cocktails with excellent Japanese whisky selection. The attention to ice, technique, and presentation reflects authentic Japanese cocktail culture.
