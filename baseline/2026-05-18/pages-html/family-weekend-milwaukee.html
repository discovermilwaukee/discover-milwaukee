FAMILY GUIDE

Milwaukee with Kids

From world-class museums to legendary frozen custard, Milwaukee is a surprisingly great family destination. Here's how to make the most of your family weekend.

Top Kid-Friendly Attractions

Milwaukee has excellent attractions for all ages. These are the must-visits for families.

Milwaukee County Zoo

All Ages

One of the country's best zoos with 2,500+ animals. The Adventure Africa exhibit, ape houses, and seasonal train rides are highlights. Plan for half a day minimum.

DON'T MISS

Feed the giraffes at the new African exhibit.

INSIDER TIP

Visit early—animals are most active in the morning. Wednesday is $4.25 admission day.

10001 W Bluemound Rd

Discovery World

Ages 3+

Hands-on science and technology museum on the lakefront. The freshwater aquarium, ship simulator, and flight exhibits are kid favorites. Easy to spend 3-4 hours here.

DON'T MISS

Touch a shark in the aquarium.

INSIDER TIP

The Denis Sullivan tall ship tours run in summer—don't miss it.

500 N Harbor Dr

Betty Brinn Children's Museum

Ages 0-10

Interactive play museum perfect for younger kids. The Word Headquarters exhibit and outdoor Pocket Park are standouts. Right next to Discovery World.

DON'T MISS

The miniature grocery store where kids can 'shop'.

INSIDER TIP

Get the combo ticket with Discovery World and save.

929 E Wisconsin Ave

Milwaukee Public Museum

All Ages

Classic natural history museum with the famous Streets of Old Milwaukee exhibit, butterfly garden, and dinosaur hall. A Milwaukee institution for generations.

DON'T MISS

Walk through the Streets of Old Milwaukee at night (it's dim inside).

INSIDER TIP

The new museum opens in 2027 near Fiserv Forum—visit the historic building while you can.

800 W Wells St

Milwaukee Art Museum

All Ages

The Calatrava wings are magical for kids. Free family programming on weekends. The Kohl's Art Generation Gallery is designed specifically for families.

DON'T MISS

Kohl's Art Generation family workshops on weekends.

INSIDER TIP

Watch the wings open at 10am—kids love it.

700 N Art Museum Dr

Family-Friendly Restaurants

Where to eat with kids in tow—no fancy tablecloths required.

Kopp's Frozen Custard

Custard & Burgers

Milwaukee's legendary frozen custard. Get the flavor of the day (it changes daily) and a Kopp's burger. Three locations—Glendale is closest to downtown.

Kid-Friendly: Walk-up window, outdoor seating, quick service.

Lakefront Brewery

Brewery/Restaurant

Family-friendly during lunch hours. Legendary Friday fish fry (served all day). Kids can have root beer while parents enjoy craft beer.

Kid-Friendly: Great outdoor space, Friday fish fry, root beer.

Blue's Egg

Breakfast/Brunch

Milwaukee's favorite brunch spot with creative dishes and a fun, bustling atmosphere. Kids menu available. Expect a wait on weekends.

Kid-Friendly: Kids menu, colorful atmosphere, hash browns for days.

Milwaukee Public Market

Food Hall

Perfect for picky eaters—everyone finds something. Cheese curds, pretzels, tacos, sushi, and more. Grab a table upstairs overlooking the market.

Kid-Friendly: Something for everyone, self-paced, cheese curds.

Sprecher Brewing

Brewery/Restaurant

Famous for their root beer and cream soda. The tour is family-friendly and includes unlimited soda samples for kids (beer for adults).

Kid-Friendly: Root beer tour, soda samples, casual atmosphere.

Outdoor Activities

Milwaukee's lakefront and parks are perfect for families who need to burn energy.

Veterans Park

Lakefront Play

Beautiful lakefront park with playgrounds, kite flying, and stunning views. The park connects to the Milwaukee Riverwalk.

PlaygroundKite flyingLakefront paths

Bradford Beach

Beach Day

Milwaukee's main beach with sand volleyball, lifeguards in summer, and food vendors. A true urban beach experience.

SwimmingSand playConcessions

Lake Park

Nature & Play

Frederick Law Olmsted-designed park with trails, a lighthouse, and excellent playgrounds. The biergarten is family-friendly.

PlaygroundLighthouseBiergarten

Bublr Bikes

Family Biking

Milwaukee's bike share system has stations throughout the city. The Oak Leaf Trail along the lakefront is flat and safe for families.
