WEEKEND GUIDE

The Perfect Milwaukee Weekend

Your complete 3-day itinerary to experience the best of Milwaukee—from world-class museums to legendary breweries, vibrant neighborhoods to unforgettable dining.

Why Milwaukee?

Milwaukee is having a moment. The NBA champion Bucks have energized downtown, the food scene rivals any coastal city, and the craft beer scene—in America's original brewing capital—is second to none. Add in stunning lakefront views, diverse neighborhoods, and Midwestern hospitality, and you've got an underrated gem that delivers way more than visitors expect. This itinerary hits all the highlights while giving you an authentic taste of what makes MKE special.

1

Day One: Art, Lakefront & Fine Dining

Morning

Milwaukee Art Museum

Start at the iconic Milwaukee Art Museum with its stunning Calatrava-designed Quadracci Pavilion. The Burke Brise Soleil 'wings' open at 10am—don't miss it. The collection spans 25,000 works.

INSIDER TIP

Free admission on the first Thursday of every month.

700 N Art Museum Dr

Lunch

Third Ward Exploration

Head to the Historic Third Ward for lunch at the Milwaukee Public Market. Browse artisan vendors, grab Foltz Family Market cheese curds, and explore the boutique shopping.

INSIDER TIP

St. Paul Fish Company has the best lobster rolls in the city.

400 N Water St

Afternoon

Lakefront Stroll

Walk the Milwaukee Riverwalk connecting downtown to the lakefront. Stop at Lakeshore State Park for stunning skyline views, then relax at Veterans Park.

INSIDER TIP

Rent a Bublr bike for a faster lakefront tour.

Milwaukee Riverwalk

Dinner

Sanford Restaurant

End day one at Sanford, Milwaukee's most acclaimed fine dining restaurant. Chef Justin Aprahamian's seasonal tasting menus are unforgettable.

INSIDER TIP

Book at least 2 weeks ahead for weekend reservations.

1547 N Jackson St

2

Day Two: Breweries & Neighborhoods

Morning

Lakefront Brewery Tour

The best brewery tour in Milwaukee—funny, informative, and includes generous samples. The fish fry is legendary. Arrive early on weekends.

INSIDER TIP

Friday fish fry is served all day and worth the wait.

1872 N Commerce St

Lunch

Bay View Dining

Head to Bay View for lunch at Odd Duck, one of Milwaukee's most creative restaurants. Small plates meant for sharing, all locally sourced.

INSIDER TIP

Sit at the bar for a more casual, walk-in friendly experience.

2352 S Kinnickinnic Ave

Afternoon

Neighborhood Hopping

Explore Bay View's vintage shops on KK, then hop to Brady Street for its eclectic mix of Italian heritage, tattoo parlors, and trendy boutiques. Stop at Glorioso's Italian Market.

INSIDER TIP

Colectivo on the lakefront is a great coffee stop.

Brady Street District

Evening

Walker's Point Nightlife

End the night in Walker's Point—Milwaukee's most diverse nightlife district. Craft cocktails at Bryant's Cocktail Lounge, then live music at The Cooperage or dancing at various clubs.

INSIDER TIP

Bryant's is cash-only and doesn't have a menu—tell them what flavors you like.

Walker's Point

3

Day Three: Culture & German Heritage

Morning

Brunch at Engine Company No. 3

Brunch in a converted firehouse with creative dishes and great cocktails. The building alone is worth the visit.

INSIDER TIP

Try the fried chicken and waffles with bourbon maple syrup.

217 W National Ave

Late Morning

Milwaukee Public Museum

Explore the Streets of Old Milwaukee exhibit, the famous butterfly garden, and world-class natural history collections. Great for all ages.

INSIDER TIP

The new museum in the Deer District opens in 2027—this historic building is worth visiting now.

800 W Wells St

Afternoon

Old World Third Street

Experience Milwaukee's German heritage. Tour the historic Pabst Brewery at Best Place, sample beer at Mader's, and grab cheese at Wisconsin Cheese Mart.

INSIDER TIP

Best Place has the best Pabst history tour—book online.

Old World Third Street

Evening

Deer District Finale

End your weekend in the Deer District. If there's a Bucks game, the energy is electric. Otherwise, enjoy dinner at Good City Brewing and drinks at the surrounding bars.

INSIDER TIP

Even without tickets, the plaza is great for people-watching on game nights.

Fiserv Forum Area
