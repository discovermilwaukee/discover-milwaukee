BEER GUIDE

Beer Lover's Milwaukee

Milwaukee was built on beer. From the historic German breweries to today's thriving craft scene, this is America's original brewing capital. Here's how to experience it all.

Milwaukee's Beer Legacy

In the late 1800s, Milwaukee's German immigrants—Pabst, Schlitz, Miller, and Blatz—made this city the beer capital of America. While consolidation changed the industry, Miller (now Molson Coors) still brews here, and the craft revolution has brought brewing back to neighborhoods across the city. Walker's Point alone has more breweries than many cities. Whether you want history or hops, Milwaukee delivers.

Historic Brewery Tours

Experience the beer that made Milwaukee famous.

Miller Brewing Company (Molson Coors)

Major Brewery Tour

Free tours of one of America's largest breweries. Walk through the caves where beer was stored before refrigeration, see the bottling line, and enjoy samples in the historic Miller Inn.

SAMPLES

3 full-size beers included

INSIDER TIP

Tours book up fast—reserve online 2+ weeks ahead. The caves are a highlight.

4251 W State St

Best Place at the Historic Pabst Brewery

Historic Brewery & Bar

The stunning former Pabst corporate headquarters, now a bar and event space. Tours tell the story of Milwaukee's brewing golden age. The Blue Ribbon Hall is gorgeous.

SAMPLES

Pabst products, craft options available

INSIDER TIP

The Pabst history tour is excellent. Friday night fish fry here is a local favorite.

901 W Juneau Ave

Craft Breweries

Milwaukee's craft scene is thriving. These are the must-visit taprooms.

Lakefront Brewery

Milwaukee's Favorite

The best brewery tour in Milwaukee—hilarious, informative, and generous with samples. The Friday fish fry is legendary (served all day Friday). A must-visit.

Try: Riverwest Stein, Eastside Dark

Weekend tours sell out—book ahead. The fish fry is worth any wait.

1872 N Commerce St

Good City Brewing

Modern Craft

Sleek Deer District taproom near Fiserv Forum. Excellent IPAs and lagers in a modern space. Perfect pre/post-game spot.

Try: Motto IPA, Risk Pils

The Deer District location is huge—easy to get a table even on game days.

333 W Juneau Ave

Third Space Brewing

German-Inspired

Award-winning German-style beers in a cozy, intimate taproom. The Happy Place Midwest Pale Ale is a local favorite. True craft quality.

Try: Happy Place MPA, Unite the Clans

Small space, so come early on weekends. Worth the trip to the Menomonee Valley.

1505 W St Paul Ave

MobCraft Beer

Experimental

Crowdsourced beer ideas voted on by fans. Expect unusual flavors and creative concepts. The Walker's Point taproom has food too.

Try: Whatever's weird this month

Check their calendar—the food options are excellent and change regularly.

505 S 5th St

Gathering Place Brewing

Neighborhood Gem

Riverwest brewery in a former hardware store. Focus on approachable, sessionable beers. Great outdoor space and community vibe.

Try: Cream City Lager

Very dog and kid friendly. The outdoor area is excellent in summer.

811 E Vienna Ave

Enlightened Brewing

Bay View Craft

Small-batch brewery focusing on Belgian and German styles. The cozy taproom in Bay View is a great neighborhood hang.

Try: Belgian Golden Strong

Limited hours—check before you go. Cash preferred.

2020 S Allis St

Component Brewing

German Precision

German and Czech-style lagers brewed with technical precision. Walker's Point location, rotating food trucks.

Try: Czech Pilsner, Munich Dunkel

Lager lovers paradise. The Czech pilsner is outstanding.

2018 S 1st St
