12 Best Thai Restaurants in Milwaukee: From Authentic to Contemporary

Milwaukee's Thai restaurant scene offers everything from family-run authentic spots to contemporary fusion. Many restaurants also feature Lao cuisine, reflecting the region's Southeast Asian community and adding dishes you won't find elsewhere.

This guide covers the best Thai restaurants in Milwaukee—from the unassuming excellence of EE-Sane to convenient downtown options. All restaurants verified open in 2026.

Most Authentic

Family-run spots with genuine flavors

EE-Sane Thai-Lao Cuisine

East Side$-$$

Thai-Lao Authentic

A quaint, family-owned restaurant that punches above its weight class. EE-Sane delivers authentic Thai and Lao cuisine with fair prices and a family-style atmosphere. The unassuming exterior hides some of Milwaukee's most genuine Southeast Asian flavors.

Must Try: Pad Thai, Larb, Thai basil dishes, Lao specialties
Best For: Authentic flavors, affordable dining, Thai-Lao exploration
Insider Tip: Don't skip the Lao dishes—they're harder to find elsewhere in Milwaukee and equally excellent.

1806 N Farwell Ave, Milwaukee, WI 53202 | Open 2026

Bangkok House

West Side$$

Traditional Thai

High-quality, authentic Thai cuisine with intensely flavored, highly creative dishes. Bangkok House uses fresh ingredients including premium spices and herbs imported directly from Thailand for genuine flavor.

Must Try: Curries, Thai basil dishes, seafood specials
Best For: Traditional Thai, quality ingredients, authentic spicing
Insider Tip: The imported Thai ingredients make a noticeable difference in authenticity.

4698 S Whitnall Ave, Milwaukee, WI 53220 | Open 2026

Mekong Cafe

Bay View$$

Thai-Lao-Vietnamese

Family-friendly restaurant offering Thai, Laotian, and Vietnamese cuisine. Co-owned by chef Banh Phongsavat, Mekong Cafe brings innovative takes on traditional Southeast Asian fare while maintaining authentic roots.

Must Try: Pho, Pad Thai, Lao specialties, Vietnamese dishes
Best For: Families, variety seekers, Bay View dining
Insider Tip: The menu spans three cuisines—take advantage and try dishes from each tradition.

2514 S Kinnickinnic Ave, Milwaukee, WI 53207 | Open 2026

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best Thai restaurant in Milwaukee?

EE-Sane Thai-Lao Cuisine on the East Side is frequently cited as the most authentic option. Bangkok House is known for using imported Thai ingredients. For convenient downtown dining, The King & I is a reliable choice.

Where can I find Lao food in Milwaukee?

EE-Sane Thai-Lao Cuisine offers both Thai and Lao dishes. Vientiane Noodle Shop specializes in Lao noodles. Mekong Cafe in Bay View also features Lao cuisine alongside Thai and Vietnamese.

What's a good Thai restaurant for spicy food?

EE-Sane and Bangkok House both offer authentic spice levels. Most Thai restaurants will adjust spice to your preference—don't be shy about asking for Thai-hot if you can handle it.

Best Thai restaurant for large groups?

Thai-namite works well for groups since it offers both Thai and sushi. Siam Thai locations typically have good seating capacity. Sweet Basil in Bay View is also group-friendly.

Where's the best Pad Thai in Milwaukee?

EE-Sane and Bangkok House are known for excellent Pad Thai with authentic technique. The King & I downtown also serves a reliable version.
