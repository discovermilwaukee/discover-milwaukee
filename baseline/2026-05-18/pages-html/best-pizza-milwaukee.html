Best Pizza in Milwaukee

The pizzerias that consistently earn top marks across all categories—quality, consistency, and atmosphere.

Santino's Little Italy

$$Tosa VillageItalian-American

Consistently ranked Milwaukee's best pizza. Santino's delivers exceptional pies in a cozy setting. The Agrigento and Diavolo pizzas get rave reviews, and everything from the sauce to the crust is made with care.

MUST TRY

Agrigento Pizza, Diavolo, House Salad

INSIDER TIP

Reservations recommended for dinner. The lunch specials are a great value.

7606 W State St, Wauwatosa, WI 53213

Zaffiro's Pizza

$$Multiple LocationsMilwaukee Tavern-Style

Milwaukee's favorite pizzeria since the 1950s. This Sicilian immigrant family has been serving thin-crust tavern-style pizza for over 70 years. The original Farwell location has the most authentic atmosphere.

MUST TRY

Classic Tavern-Style with Sausage, Pepperoni

INSIDER TIP

The original Farwell location is tiny—go early or expect a wait.

1724 N Farwell Ave (Original), plus downtown and suburbs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is tavern-style pizza?

Tavern-style pizza is a Milwaukee/Midwest tradition featuring a thin, cracker-like crust that's crispy and sturdy enough to hold toppings without flopping. It's typically cut into squares (party cut) rather than triangles. This style originated in the taverns of Milwaukee and Chicago, designed to be eaten while drinking at the bar.

What is the best pizza in Milwaukee?

Top-rated Milwaukee pizzerias include Santino's Little Italy (best overall), San Giorgio Pizzeria Napoletana (best Neapolitan), Zaffiro's (classic Milwaukee tavern-style), and Transfer Pizzeria (cracker crust). The best pizza depends on your preferred style—Milwaukee has excellent options across all categories.

Where can I get New York style pizza in Milwaukee?

Classic Slice in Bay View is the go-to for NY-style by-the-slice pizza with large, foldable slices. For full pies, several spots offer NY-influenced styles, though Milwaukee's native tavern-style is more common.

What is Milwaukee known for in pizza?

Milwaukee is known for tavern-style pizza—thin, crispy crust cut into squares (party cut). Zaffiro's, opened in the 1950s by Sicilian immigrants, is considered the quintessential Milwaukee pizzeria. The style is meant to be shared with drinks in a tavern setting.

Where is the best Neapolitan pizza in Milwaukee?

San Giorgio Pizzeria Napoletana in the Third Ward is widely considered the best Neapolitan pizza in Milwaukee. Their wood-fired oven and imported ingredients produce authentic Naples-style pizza. Fixture Pizza Pub in Bay View is another excellent option.

Is there Detroit-style pizza in Milwaukee?

Yes! Brute Pizza in Walker's Point specializes in Detroit-style pizza with that signature thick, airy crust and caramelized cheese edges (frico). They've developed a cult following for their unique approach.
